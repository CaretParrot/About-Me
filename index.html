About Me

Bio

Hello there! I'm Rishi. I am an aspiring web and software developer, music composer, violist, and chess enthusiast. I am also the founder of The Composers Foundry, a community dedicated to growing the music making community and providing assistance to those who wish to get into the hobby.

Developing programs has always piqued my interest ever since I was little, and I hope to continue to create computer programs for the forseeable future. It can often be an arduous process that can even take several months, but it has always felt great to use a program that functions smoothly and is designed with the user in mind first. Furthermore, the possibilities are endless, so it's a hobby I can maintain even as my other interests change over time. In fact, the website you see in front of you was coded from the ground up by me! Throughout 2024, I have achieved industry certifications for Computer Programming I, Computer Science Principles, and Web Development I.

Soon after the days where I dreamed about the possibilities of computer programming as a young kid, I also took up the viola. Through music I found a welcoming and energetic community and have received vast amounts of support for my musical education. Because of this, I have been the principal violist at my high school orchestra, scored top marks for solo and ensemble performances in my district and state, and have played in district and state orchestral ensembles for many years.

I also taught myself how to write music when I was younger as well. Originally I did it as an experiment to see if I was interested in the hobby, but eventually it became a way for me to escape the challenging times of the pandemic and the business of high school life. Despite having taken next to no classes in composition or theory, my music writing skills grew thanks to the help of my orchestra teacher, and I won the 2023 and 2024 KMEA Jeff Jordan Composition Competitions.

Unlike the previous hobbies, chess is a very recent one. I learned how to play chess when I was much younger, but I never took chess training seriously until the middle of 2023. In one year, I gained over 800 rapid rating points on chess.com and have reached a peak rating of 1703. I hope to continue improving at chess in the future!
